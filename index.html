The problem

Free AI training has done its job: most professionals now know what AI is. Very few run any part of their week differently because of it. A certificate of awareness changes nothing about the report you still write by hand, the inbox you still clear line by line, or the briefing that still takes you Thursday and Friday.

The gap isn't knowledge. It's capability on your work — and capability is built by doing your actual tasks, with someone holding you to a standard, and a measurement at the end that tells you honestly whether anything moved.

That's what this course is.

What you'll do

You bring five recurring tasks from your own job. Before the first session, we baseline what they cost you in hours. Then, across six live sessions over three weeks, you learn one working method — specify, generate, verify, refine — and apply it to those tasks, week by week:

  1. Directing AI — set up your assistant, connect it to your first real material, and learn the method you'll use for everything else.
  2. AI for communication — emails, client responses, internal updates: briefed to your standard, checked by you.
  3. Documents and reporting — meeting summaries, reports, rewrites — including the drill where you learn to catch confident, well-formatted, wrong output before it ships.
  4. Connecting real systems — give your assistant access to the documents and tools your work actually lives in, with the governance to do it safely.
  5. Automating a real workflow — your capstone: one of your own five tasks, rebuilt end to end.
  6. Standardise and present — turn what worked into reusable templates, re-measure your five tasks, and present the result: hours before, hours after, and why it moved.

At every step, the judgment stays with you. The assistant does the work; you own the standard and the send button.

What you leave with

  • A measured before/after on your own five tasks — hours per month, honestly counted (we include the time you spend checking the output, not just generating it).
  • A working set of reusable templates and briefs for your recurring tasks — yours to keep and reuse the following Monday.
  • The Certified AI at Work Practitioner credential, backed by that measurement — not by attendance.

The evidence

Independent studies show roughly 14–40% on this kind of work — we measure yours. We quote that range as evidence, never as a guarantee: your number comes off your own five tasks, and if nothing moved, you'll know — because we measure it.

One example of what the gap looks like in practice: at a large SA agribusiness group, a ~30-hour senior research-and-briefing task collapsed to an afternoon via two ~30-line prompt files. Same person, same task, same standard — different method.

Who it's for — and who it isn't

This course is for you if

your week contains recurring, document- and communication-heavy work — reports, briefs, client emails, summaries, research — and you're an SME owner, manager, or professional willing to put your real tasks on the table and be measured on them.

It is not for you if

your core work is open-ended expert judgment with no recurring pattern — the research is clear that AI doesn't reliably help there, and we won't pretend otherwise. It's also not for you if you want a recorded overview to watch later: this is live practice, not content. If that's what you need, the free vendor courses are genuinely good — start there and come back when you want your Tuesday to change.

FAQ

We use Copilot at work — is this still relevant?

Yes. The skill you're building is directing an AI assistant to a professional standard — brief it, check its work, refine it. That method transfers across assistants. We teach on Claude because its workspace tooling lets you connect your real documents and build reusable workflows within the course, but graduates apply the same method to whatever their employer runs.

Is this technical? I don't have an IT background.

No code, no technical background needed. If you can brief a capable colleague — say what you want, what standard it must hit, and check the result — you can do everything in this course. Your work expertise is the input; the course adds the direction skill.

Why isn't it recorded?

Because a recording of someone else practising is exactly the free-course model — awareness without capability. The value is in doing your own tasks live, being challenged on your brief, and catching errors under supervision. We keep the seats limited for the same reason.

Can my employer pay?

Yes — we invoice on registration (prices ex-VAT), and many participants have their seat covered as professional development. If you're thinking of sending several people, book the 45-minute team demo instead: the team format includes a measured report to your sponsor.

What AI tool do I need, and what does it cost me?

A Claude Pro subscription (~R370/month), which you set up before Session 1 with our pre-course guide. You bring your own seat so everything you build — templates, briefs, workflows — lives in your own account and keeps working after the course ends.

Do I get a certificate?

Yes: Certified AI at Work Practitioner, issued on completion. It's backed by your own measured before/after result, not attendance — which is also what makes it worth showing an employer.

What if I can't attend a session?

With 90-minute sessions twice a week, most people manage all six. If you miss one, you'll get the session brief and the application task, and we'll help you catch up in the next session. If you know upfront you'll miss more than one, take a seat on the next cohort instead — you'll get more from it.
