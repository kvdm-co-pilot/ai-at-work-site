The Task-Fit Filter

Is this a workflow AI will actually win at? A one-page screen for choosing a task before you invest a brief in it — used to pick a capstone in the AI at Work course, and useful the moment you read it.

Step 1 — Two hard gates

These aren't scored. If the answer is "yes" to either, the task is off-frontier as written — stop and reshape it.

G1 — Judgement: Are you asking the AI to make the final expert judgement (decide strategy, diagnose, rule on a case, sign off) rather than to do the work that informs your judgement? → off-frontier.

G2 — Stakes: Is the output high-stakes and irreversible with no human check before it lands (money moves, legal filing, a decision sent to a client/board unreviewed)? → off-frontier until a human gate is added.

Both gates encode the same rule: the AI does the work; the human owns the judgement and the send button.

Step 2 — Score the fit

Score each question 0 = no · 1 = partly · 2 = yes.

#QuestionScore
1Bounded — is there a clear "done"? You could describe a good result in a sentence. 
2Document / comms / data-shaped — is the core reading, writing, summarising, or reformatting text or structured data? 
3Recurring — does it happen often (daily/weekly)? 
4Verifiable fast — can you check the output against a clear standard in minutes? 
5Inputs reachable — does the AI have (or can a connector give it) the material it needs? 
6Judgement-light at the core — is the thinking mostly assembly/transformation, with you keeping the calls? 
Total (max 12) 

Step 3 — Verdict

ScoreVerdictDo this
9–12On-frontier — proceed.This is a strong candidate. Baseline the hours before you start.
5–8Reshape it (Step 4).Narrow it until it scores green — don't abandon it.
0–4Pick a different task.Or carve one on-frontier sub-task out of it.

Step 4 — Reshape an off-frontier task

Most "too big" tasks contain a great sub-task: decompose → let the AI do the assembly → you keep the judgement.

Off-frontier (as stated)On-frontier reshape
"Decide our Q3 strategy" (G1 fail — judgement)"Assemble a cited briefing pack on the three options; I decide."
"Handle the client relationship" (too open)"Draft the weekly client update from the activity log; I review and send."
"Do our compliance" (high-stakes, vague)"List which documents are outstanding and draft the chasers; I approve."
"Write the board report" (judgement + stakes)"Turn the ops figures into a formatted draft with a 'needs a decision' box; I finalise."

Worked examples

  • Green. "Draft the weekly board email from the ops summary." Bounded (2), doc-shaped (2), weekly (2), verifiable (2), inputs in hand (2), judgement-light (2) = 12. Both gates pass. Strong candidate.
  • Amber. "Keep the team across industry news." Scores 9 but vague — reshape to "a cited weekly briefing on these 5 topics, flagged by confidence" → clean green.
  • Red. "Approve or deny claims automatically." G1 and G2 both fail (final judgement + irreversible, high stakes). Reshape to "pre-screen and summarise each claim against the checklist; a human decides" — and still treat AI-driven denial as a flagged governance risk.
